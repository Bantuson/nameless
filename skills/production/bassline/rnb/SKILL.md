## Default — act on this

Default approach: Before writing a bassline, find the key by playing notes on the keyboard and keeping the ones that fit, then establish the root notes for the bassline. Keep the bassline within the natural minor scale, departing only for the harmonic-minor sharp seven — anywhere you place notes within the scale template will sound right — and add two-five-one runs — they are synonymous with R&B.

### key-finding — 1 source(s) agree
Before writing a bassline, find the key by playing notes on the keyboard and keeping the ones that fit. If the track sounds like it is in a minor key, use the relative minor of the major key whose notes fit.

### root-notes — 1 source(s) agree
After establishing the key, establish the root notes for the bassline. Bass notes need not match the chord's root — the bass, being the lowest note, strongly influences how the chord sounds.

### leading-notes — 1 source(s) agree
Play the semitone below the note you want to arrive at as a leading note into it. You can also lead into a target note from the semitone above, leading down.

### two-five-one — 1 source(s) agree
Add two-five-one runs to the bassline — they are synonymous with R&B. Play the two-five-one anywhere on the keyboard — it is always relative to the landing note, and it is the same whether you take it from the major or the minor scale of the landing note. Do a run into a two-five-one — it gives the two-five-one that much more impact.
