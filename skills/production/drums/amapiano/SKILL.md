## Default — act on this

Default approach: Add percussion elements specifically to create the groove. Insert a guide loop first, then create your percussion around it — pick a guide loop whose bounce you like, and check that the inserted loop is on time with the project before building on it. Treat sound selection as the critical step when building percussion around a loop: listen carefully to the guide loop, check for similar sounds, and use those to build on top of it. Once the loop has established the bounds of the rhythm, remove it and continue building your own percussion. Route all percussion elements into a single bus, level them so they don't fight for space, and remove the percussion's low frequencies with a parametric EQ to create space for the kick and the log drum.
