## Default — act on this

Default approach: Use single-track vocals during verses and bring in the doubles or triples during the choruses to elevate the energy. Fix a technically fine but weak-feeling lead vocal by double tracking: record the exact same vocal line a second time and layer it on top of the original take, with both takes panned dead center. Only add vocal layers intentionally — being able to stack more vocals does not mean you should; for vulnerable, raw, or deeply personal songs, use fewer vocal layers — less is often more.

### gang-vocals — 1 source(s) agree
Record gang vocals by yourself using around eight to 10 takes, intentionally singing each gang-vocal take differently — with different tones, volumes, and characters.
