## Default — act on this

Default approach: Play a new musical idea two times in total to reinforce it — once is not enough for the brain to truly remember it — but do not repeat a musical idea beyond three times; that is overusing it. On the third time, go somewhere musically different: a new melody with the same chords, or a new progression with a new melody. The source also offers an alternative camp — start the third repetition the same but go somewhere different partway through the idea or progression. This default picks the new melody or new progression camp because it keeps the arrangement from being fully predictable — if the brain always knows where the music is going, there is no reason to keep listening.
