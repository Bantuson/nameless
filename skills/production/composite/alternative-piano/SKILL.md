## Grounding — how this skill was derived

**Decomposed into parent techniques** (this subgenre is under-tutorialized, so it is composed from its taught parents, never fabricated):
- **amapiano log-drum groove** (amapiano · drums) — the rhythmic foundation — the log drum and its swing — but softer and rounder, carrying momentum without dominating the mix.
- **jazzy / soulful extended piano voicings** (rnb · chords) — the harmonic identity — lush, extended (7th/9th) voicings that leave space for each element, the private-school discipline.
- **deep-house space and dub** (deep-house · atmosphere) — the air around the parts — delay/dub sends, airy pads and panned organic percussion that open up the arrangement.

**Negative space** (what this subgenre deliberately omits or breaks vs its parents — often the real identity):
- Sparser than mainstream amapiano: space is left for each instrument and the vocal — 'less nightclub, more sophisticated lounge'.
- Slower and more soulful than club deep house — a relaxed lane, not a peak-time pump.
- The log drum is present but deliberately softer/rounder than mainstream amapiano; it is not the loudest element.
